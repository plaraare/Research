Featured publication

Gold nanoparticle based double-labeling of melanoma extracellular vesicles to determine the specificity of uptake by cells and preferential accumulation in small metastatic lung tumors

One of key problems of metastasis is the lack of drugs able to target the small secondary tumor nodules.  My study  demonstrated that melanoma extracellular vesicles (EVs) act as natural nanoparticles and have a preferential uptake towards melanoma metastasis. The publication is highly cited (46 citations in less than two years) and describes the potential of EVs as trojan horses for cancer therapeutics

Featured publication

M1-derived extracellular vesicles enhance photodynamic therapy and promote immunological memory in preclinical models of colon cancer

This work shows different antitumoral capacity of EVs according to their physiological states. EVs from activated immune cells were able to induce Dendric cell maturation, reduce cell growth and promote a vaccine-like cancer immunological memory after PDT. Modulating the physiological state of EVs for optimal antitumoral efficacy is one of the key concepts of my research

Featured project

Therasomes: Theranostic exosomes for photodynamic therapy and immunotherapy of cancer 

Granted KWF project Application Type: Research Project: Call Number: 2020-1  

This proposal aim to develop a novel drug delivery platform using theranostic exosomes (“Therasomes”) for simultaneous immunotherapy, tumor targeting, photodynamic therapy (PDT) and imaging of cancer. To achieve this, tumor antigen presenting exosomes will be simultaneously loaded with photosensitizers (PS) and upconversion nanoparticles (UCNPs), which will be externally activated for deep tissue PDT

S​1. WO2018122598 - CURCUMIN-LOADED NANOEMULSIONS, METHOD OF MANUFACTURE, AND METHOD OF PREVENTIVE TREATMENT USING THE SAME

Read More


 A oil-in-water curcumin nanoemulsion that includes curcumin dissolved in at least one miscible solvent and encapsulated in an oil core, wherein the oil core also comprises a stabilizer, wherein the oil core forms an organic phase which is dispersed in an aqueous solvent, and wherein the oil core is selected from a pharmaceutically acceptable oil. A method of manufacturing an oil-in-water curcumin nanoemulsion includes dissolving the curcumin in at least one miscible solvent; encapsulating the curcumin in the oil core to produce an organic phase solution; adding the stabilizer to the organic phase solution; dispersing the organic phase solution in the aqueous solvent; and evaporating the mixture. A method of preventing metastatic cancer using an oil-in-water curcumin nanoemulsion by administering an amount of the nanoemulsion topically to an area of an excised primary tumor, and monitoring any reincidence of metastatic cancer in the excised primary tumor area.
